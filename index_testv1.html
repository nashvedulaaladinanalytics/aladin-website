FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Aladin™ a trading platform?

No. Aladin™ is decision infrastructure. We do not execute trades or provide signals. The platform delivers state-aware intelligence and governance-ready research so institutions can act with discipline — or stand down with confidence when market structure is impaired.

Why does Aladin™ institutional engagement matter?

Global refineries actively subscribe to Aladin's monthly and enterprise-grade research coverage, with ongoing enterprise discussions involving institutional banks, metals desks, and hedge funds.

Dubai supports tens of billions of dollars in annual precious-metals inflows and outflows and facilitates a significant share of global physical gold trade each year — making institutional standards, adoption discipline, and execution integrity essential.

Institutional engagement reflects operational relevance and governance alignment, not retail popularity.

How does Aladin™ justify value without making performance claims?

Aladin™ does not forecast prices or promise returns. It focuses on identifying when market conditions are structurally sound — and when restraint is warranted due to degraded price discovery, dealer behavior, or liquidity stress.

In practice, institutions often justify the subscription by avoiding a small number of low-quality execution windows over the course of a year.

How is Aladin™ intelligence delivered and controlled?

Intelligence is delivered via secure, DRM-controlled distribution with watermarking, access controls, and tier-based permissions.

Access privileges are tiered by subscription level as a governance measure — with higher institutional tiers granted limited download rights to support internal compliance workflows, while standard tiers maintain controlled access to preserve distribution integrity and auditability.

How should institutions think about the cost of Aladin™?

Subscription costs typically represent approximately two basis points of annual turnover for active market participants — positioning Aladin™ as an operational intelligence expense, not a speculative cost.

The platform is designed to support execution discipline and capital preservation, rather than continuous activity.

Why are access controls and user limits imposed?

Access controls exist for governance, auditability, and risk-control purposes — not as feature restrictions. Controlled distribution ensures intelligence quality, protects market integrity, and maintains the institutional standards required by DMCC-licensed operators and sovereign-grade clients.
